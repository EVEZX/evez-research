## Incident

**Date:** March 2, 2023
**Location:** Ogden, UT — Union Pacific mainline
**FRA Record:** UP0323RM001
**Severity:** 37 hazmat cars in consist, 12 derailed, $648,751 damage
**Chemicals:** UP stated magnesium chloride; witness observed possible cyclohexane
**Full manifest:** NOT in FRA database (UP entered "N/A"; database defaulted to 0 hazmat)

## Why NTSB Was Not Notified

A three-layer suppression mechanism prevented NTSB involvement:
1. FRA Form 54 does not collect car-level hazmat consist data
2. UP entered "N/A" for hazmat classification on the form
3. The FRA database defaulted the hazmat count to "0"
4. This prevented automatic PHMSA/EPA/NTSB notification triggers

## Interstate Impact

The chemical plume crossed state lines into Wyoming, covering I-80 mile markers 15-33 (Evanston/Fort Bridger area). No air monitoring, no evacuation, no hazmat response was conducted in Wyoming. Hundreds of elk died in the corridor. Elk meat was distributed to a food bank without testing.

## Request

1. NTSB should investigate why this incident was not referred for investigation
2. NTSB should obtain the full hazmat consist from UP internal records
3. NTSB should examine the FRA Form 54 data collection gap that allowed this suppression
4. NTSB should issue a safety recommendation regarding hazmat classification reporting on FRA Form 54
