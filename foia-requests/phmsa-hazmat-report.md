## Incident Summary

**Date of Incident:** March 2, 2023
**Location:** Ogden, UT (Union Pacific rail yard/mainline)
**Railroad:** Union Pacific Railroad
**FRA Record:** UP0323RM001
**Cars Involved:** 37 hazmat cars in consist, 12 cars derailed
**Damage:** $648,751 (per FRA Form 54)

## Chemicals Involved

- **UP stated:** Magnesium chloride
- **Witness observation:** Possible cyclohexane or other volatile organic compound
- **Full manifest:** NOT publicly available. UP entered "N/A" for hazmat classification on FRA Form 54. Full car-level consist exists only in UP internal records.

## Plume Migration

The chemical plume from the derailment site crossed state lines into Wyoming, covering I-80 mile markers 15-33 (Evanston/Fort Bridger area). No air quality monitoring was conducted. No evacuation was ordered. No hazmat response was initiated on the Wyoming side.

## Environmental Impact

- Hundreds of elk died in the plume corridor (Uinta County, WY)
- No necropsy or toxicology testing was performed on elk carcasses
- WGFD attributed deaths to "winter kill" without laboratory analysis
- Elk carcasses were donated to the Rock Springs, WY food bank and distributed as food WITHOUT chemical contamination testing

## Suppression Mechanism

1. FRA Form 54 does not collect car-level hazmat consist data
2. UP entered "N/A" for hazmat classification
3. Database defaulted to "0" hazmat cars
4. This prevented PHMSA, EPA, NTSB, and Wyoming DEQ from triggering investigation protocols
5. No federal agency was automatically notified of the hazmat release

## Requested Action

1. **Obtain full car-level hazmat consist** from Union Pacific internal records for train involved in UP0323RM001
2. **Initiate retroactive hazmat incident investigation** for the March 2, 2023 Ogden derailment
3. **Coordinate with EPA Region 8** regarding interstate chemical plume migration into Wyoming
4. **Coordinate with USFWS/WGFD** regarding elk mortality event and food bank contamination
