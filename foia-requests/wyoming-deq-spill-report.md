## Spill/Release Description

In late February/early March 2023, a chemical plume from a Union Pacific train derailment at Ogden, UT (March 2, 2023, FRA record UP0323RM001) crossed state lines into Wyoming. The plume covered I-80 mile markers 15-33 near Evanston and Fort Bridger.

- No air quality monitoring was conducted
- No evacuation was ordered
- No hazmat response was initiated in Wyoming
- No spill report was filed with Wyoming DEQ (to my knowledge)

## Environmental Impact

- Hundreds of elk died in the Uinta County corridor during this period
- WGFD attributed deaths to "winter kill" without laboratory analysis
- No toxicology testing was performed on elk carcasses
- Elk carcasses were donated to the Rock Springs food bank and distributed as food WITHOUT chemical testing

## Chemicals

- UP stated: Magnesium chloride
- Witness observation: Possible cyclohexane or other volatile organic compound
- Full hazmat manifest: Only in UP internal records (not in FRA/PHMSA databases)

## Request

1. Wyoming DEQ should investigate the interstate chemical plume migration
2. Wyoming DEQ should request the full hazmat manifest from Union Pacific
3. Wyoming DEQ should coordinate with WGFD regarding elk toxicology
4. Wyoming DEQ should assess whether elk meat distributed through the Rock Springs food bank poses a public health risk
