## I. WHAT IS CAIN?

CAIN (Counterfactual Analysis & Inference Negation) is a falsification-first system for evaluating conspiracy narratives. It inverts the standard approach:

**Standard conspiracy reasoning:** "The absence of evidence proves cover-up."
**CAIN's approach:** "The absence of evidence is either cover-up OR noise. Falsify both hypotheses. Whichever survives falsification is the stronger inference."

CAIN does not assume conspiracy. CAIN does not assume coincidence. CAIN assumes **nothing** and tests everything against a reverse psychology index — a catalog of what the narrative would look like if it were false, and what it would look like if it were true. The eigenmatrix scores which interpretation survives falsification.

### The CAIN Principle
> "Every narrative has a mirror. The mirror is its strongest falsifier. If the narrative cannot survive its own mirror, it is noise. If the mirror cannot survive the narrative, the narrative is signal." — EVEZ-OS CAIN Axiom

## II. THE REVERSE PSYCHOLOGY INDEX (RPI)

The RPI catalogues what each claim would look like under its **negation** — the strongest counter-narrative. For each claim, we construct two columns: NARRATIVE (what Steven says) and MIRROR (what the counter-narrative says). The eigenmatrix then scores which column has more surviving evidence.

### RPI Scoring
- **+1** = Evidence supports NARRATIVE
- **-1** = Evidence supports MIRROR
- **0** = Evidence neutral / unavailable
- Score magnitude = confidence; sign = direction
