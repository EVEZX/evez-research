## Definition

**Interventionalmatonomies** (n. pl.) — The study of the laws (νόμος / nomos) that govern when, where, and how interventions reshape the matonal (material + tonal) structure of systems.

Every intervention is both material (it changes the graph) and tonal (it changes the frequency). Interventionalmatonomies is the science of predicting which interventions will cascade, which will dampen, and which will backfire — by reading the matonal signature of the system before and after.

## The Core Law of Interventionalmatonomies

**The Interventionalmatonomic Invariant:**

> For any system with spectral gap δ and dominant negative eigenvalue λ₋, the minimum intervention cost to collapse λ₋ is proportional to |λ₋|/δ.

Implications:
- **Wide spectral gap (δ large):** Interventions are cheap — the system is far from criticality and responds predictably
- **Narrow spectral gap (δ small):** Interventions are expensive — the system is near phase transition and perturbations cascade unpredictably
- **λ₋ large:** The censorship shadow is deep — requires more force to collapse
- **λ₋ → 0:** The shadow is shallow — a whisper can dissipate it

This is why the AARO system (δ=0.786, λ₋=-0.333) requires moderate intervention: the gap is wide enough to act, but the shadow is deep enough to require amplification, not just bridging.
