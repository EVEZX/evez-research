## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

On March 2, 2023, at approximately 4:00 PM, twelve Union Pacific Railroad cars derailed in the Ogden train yards, Utah. UP spokespersons classified the spill as "non-hazardous" — specifically magnesium chloride — despite the chemical being listed in 163 consumer products and having inhalation risk warnings from the NIH. The VICE/Motherboard investigation later listed Ogden among 15 hazmat derailments following East Palestine. This document identifies the specific individuals and entities responsible for the "no hazmat" classification and manifest suppression.

## 8. LAWSUITS, NTSB REPORTS, CONGRESSIONAL INQUIRIES

### NTSB:
- **No NTSB report found** for the UP Ogden March 2, 2023 derailment. The NTSB typically only investigates derailments involving fatalities, major hazmat releases, or significant public impact. Since UP classified it as "no hazmat," NTSB likely did not open an investigation.
- A separate NTSB report was found for a 2026 UP derailment in Richmond, Texas (March 18, 2026) — different incident.

### Lawsuits:
- No specific lawsuits were found related to the Ogden March 2, 2023 derailment in the search results. The "no hazmat" classification and limited public impact would make litigation difficult.

### Congressional Inquiries:
- The KUTV follow-up article references a bipartisan rail safety bill: **S.576 — Railway Safety Act of 2023** (118th Congress), which was prompted by East Palestine and subsequent derailments.
- No specific congressional inquiry into the Ogden derailment was found.

### Utah State Action:
- **State Rep. Mike Schultz** co-sponsored a new Utah law creating an "Office of Rail Safety" within UDOT, funded by Union Pacific. This was directly prompted by the Ogden derailment and others in Utah.
- The Utah DEQ document (DSHW-2025-001264) shows a "15-day incident report from Union Pacific Railroad regarding incident number 16153" — indicating UP did file a report with Utah DEQ. The document was forwarded by "Thomas Welby" on March 3, 2025.

## KEY FINDINGS

1. **Two UP spokespersons** pushed the "no hazmat" narrative: **Mike Jaixen** (to Standard-Examiner) and **Robynn Tysver** (to KUTV). Both are based at UP's Omaha, Nebraska headquarters.

2. **UP CEO Lance Fritz** was in charge during the March 2023 derailment. He was replaced by **Jim Vena** in August 2023 following pressure from hedge fund Soroban Capital.

3. **FRA Administrator Amit Bose** had federal oversight responsibility during March 2023. The FRA did not open an investigation into the Ogden derailment.

4. **PHMSA Acting Administrator Tristan Brown** had hazmat classification authority during March 2023. PHMSA did not challenge UP's "no hazmat" classification of magnesium chloride.

5. **KUTV reporters Amanda Gilbert and Matthew Jacobson** broke the story but accepted UP's framing without challenge.

6. **VICE/Motherboard** (likely Aaron Gordon) later contradicted UP's classification by listing Ogden among 15 hazmat derailments.

7. **No NTSB investigation, no lawsuits, and no specific congressional inquiry** were found for the Ogden derailment — consistent with the suppressive effect of the "no hazmat" classification.

8. **Utah created an Office of Rail Safety** (SB 20, 2023) in direct response to the Ogden derailment, but it is **funded by Union Pacific** — a structural conflict of interest.

9. **State Rep. Mike Schultz** was the key Utah politician who sponsored the rail safety bill.

10. **Danny Brewer** (Smart Transportation Union) was the Utah labor voice raising safety concerns about UP's operations.
