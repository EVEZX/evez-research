# OSINT INVESTIGATION: Spruce Pine HPQ Corporate Network
## The World's Most Critical Semiconductor Supply Chokepoint

**Investigation Date:** 2026-06-28
**Classification:** OSINT — Open Source Intelligence
**Subject:** Corporate control of high-purity quartz (HPQ) at Spruce Pine, NC

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The world's supply of high-purity quartz (HPQ) — the irreplaceable material for semiconductor crucibles and solar photovoltaic manufacturing — is controlled by **two companies** operating in a single geological district in Spruce Pine, North Carolina (population: 2,194). BloombergNEF estimates Spruce Pine produces **80% of the world's HPQ** needed for semiconductor and solar industries. The two operators are:

1. **Sibelco** (Belgian) — the larger producer, operating through its subsidiary Covia (formerly Unimin)
2. **The Quartz Corp** (Norwegian) — the smaller producer, owned by Imerys (France) and Norsk Mineral (Norway)

This is what Ed Conway, author of *Material World*, calls "the single point of failure" for the global semiconductor supply chain.

## 5. CONNECTIONS TO SEMICONDUCTOR INDUSTRY

### Direct Connections
- **No direct board-level connections** between Sibelco/Quartz Corp directors and TSMC, Intel, Samsung, or ASML were identified in public records.
- The HPQ business is inherently connected to the semiconductor supply chain, but the relationship is **customer-supplier**, not cross-ownership.

### Key Semiconductor Customers (HPQ Consumers)
The HPQ from Spruce Pine flows to:
1. **Polysilicon producers** (who melt silicon in quartz crucibles):
   - Wacker Chemie (Germany)

   - GlobalWafers (Taiwan)
   - Siltronic (Germany)
   - SK Siltron (South Korea)
3. **End semiconductor manufacturers** (who depend on wafers):
   - **TSMC** (Taiwan) — world's largest contract chipmaker
   - **Samsung** (South Korea)
   - **Intel** (USA)
   - **Micron** (USA)
   - **SMIC** (China)

### Supply Chain Flow
```
Spruce Pine HPQ → Crucible manufacturers (Japan: Shin-Etsu, etc.)
  → Polysilicon producers (Wacker, GCL, Hemlock, etc.)

```

## 6. HURRICICANE HELENE — THE SHUTDOWN

### Timeline
- **September 26, 2024:** Both Sibelco and The Quartz Corp halted all operations at Spruce Pine
- Hurricane dumped 24+ inches of rain in 24 hours on the town
- The North Toe River rose approximately 6 meters (20 feet), flooding processing facilities located on the riverbank
- Roads, railways, power lines, water treatment, and communications infrastructure destroyed

### Who Made the Decision
- **Sibelco** issued a statement on September 30 confirming the shutdown. The decision was made by **Sibelco corporate management** (CEO Hilmar Rode and EVP North America Paolo Gennari) in coordination with local site management.
- **The Quartz Corp** issued a statement on October 1. The decision was made by Quartz Corp management.
- Both shutdowns were **forced/voluntary** — the hurricane made operations physically impossible (no power, no road access, flooding), but the companies explicitly chose to halt for employee safety before the worst impact.

### Damage Assessment
- **Sibelco (Oct 4 update):** "Initial assessment indicates that our operating facilities in the Spruce Pine region have only sustained **minor damage**." Final product stock was NOT impacted. Power restoration was the critical bottleneck.
- **The Quartz Corp (Oct 2):** Plants "do not seem to have been seriously damaged" but "too early to tell" when operations would resume due to infrastructure rebuild.
- **John Walker (former Quartz Corp CEO):** Estimated up to 6 months for full recovery, noting flooded equipment, silt contamination in HPQ facilities, and the need to replace pumps, motors, and gearboxes.

### Financial Impact
- Sibelco's H1 2025 results explicitly stated the HPQ business was "still recovering from Hurricane Helene" and was "also impacted by ongoing US-China tariff negotiations."
- The company had planned to **double production capacity at Spruce Pine by 2025** — this expansion was delayed.
- Sibelco 2025 full-year results acknowledged "strong financial results despite a highly challenging global backdrop" with HPQ specifically called out.
- Exact revenue impact is not publicly disclosed (private company), but HPQ is one of three strategic pillars — the shutdown likely cost tens of millions in lost revenue.
- For context: HPQ sells for ~$10,000/ton; Spruce Pine produces 180-200,000 tons/year = approximately $1.8-2 billion in annual HPQ value across both companies. Even a few weeks of shutdown represents tens of millions in lost revenue.
- The **2008 fire** at Spruce Pine caused polysilicon price spikes, demonstrating systemic fragility.

### Recovery
- Mining operations partially resumed by mid-October 2024
- The Quartz Corp reported safety stockpiles in "different locations" prevented short-term supply disruption
- Industry stockpiles of crucibles provided 2-3 month cushion

## 8. HISTORY OF HPQ MINING AT SPRUCE PINE

### Geological Origin
- Spruce Pine Mining District formed ~370-380 million years ago when Africa collided with North America (Taconic Orogeny)
- The pegmatites were dated to ~336 million years ago (40Ar/39Ar dating)
- The district is ~25 miles long and 5 miles wide along the North Toe River valley
- Purity is due to **lack of water** during mineral formation, preventing impurity introduction

### Mining History
- **Pre-colonial:** Native Americans mined mica from deep tunnels in the mountainside
- **1907:** Town of Spruce Pine founded with the Clinchfield Railroad's arrival
- **Early 20th century:** The Feldspar Company and Spruce Pine Mica established major mining operations
- **1879:** Thomas Edison used Spruce Pine mica to insulate wiring — first electronics application
- **1970s:** Sibelco entered Spruce Pine through **acquisition of Unimin** (North America's largest silica sand producer)
- Sibelco invested in new extraction and processing technologies, establishing the IOTA® HPQ brand
- The Quartz Corp was formed as a JV to consolidate Imerys' and Norsk Mineral's HPQ assets

### Key Historical Players
- **The Feldspar Corporation** — early mining operator
- **Spruce Pine Mica** — early mica mining
- **Unimin** — acquired by Sibelco in the 1970s, became Covia
- **K-T Feldspar** — local operator
- **US Gypsum** — operated in district
- **Covia** (formerly Unimin) — Sibelco's US subsidiary

### August 4, 2007
Arsonist set fire to several downtown Spruce Pine buildings, severely damaging the area.

### 2008
A fire at a Spruce Pine quartz facility caused **polysilicon price spikes** globally — demonstrating the systemic fragility of the single-source supply chain.

## 10. STRATEGIC ASSESSMENT

### Key Findings

1. **Concentrated Belgian Family Control:** Sibelco is controlled by a network of Belgian industrial families through holding vehicles. The 2024 buyback of 18.93% of shares and expulsion of the LL/Quarzwerke group consolidated family control further.

2. **GBL Nexus:** The Quartz Corp's ultimate parent, Imerys, is controlled by Groupe Bruxelles Lambert — one of Europe's oldest and most powerful holding companies, with the Frère and Desmarais families at its core. This connects HPQ to a vast portfolio including Adidas, Pernod Ricard, SGS, and more.

3. **No Semiconductor Industry Cross-Ownership:** Despite the critical dependency, there are no equity or board-level links between the HPQ producers and the semiconductor manufacturers (TSMC, Intel, Samsung, ASML). The relationship is purely commercial supplier-customer.

4. **Single Point of Failure:** Two companies, one geological district, ~80-90% of world supply. Hurricane Helene proved this vulnerability is not theoretical.

5. **Secrecy:** The HPQ industry is described as "extremely secretive" by analysts. Specific production volumes, pricing, and contract terms are not publicly disclosed.

6. **Recovery Vulnerability:** Sibelco's 2025 annual report confirms HPQ business was still recovering from Hurricane Helene AND impacted by US-China tariff negotiations — a dual shock.

7. **Strategic Appointments:** Sibelco created a dedicated EVP HPQ role (Nick Traber, June 2025) post-Helene, signaling recognition of the strategic criticality of this business segment.

8. **No US Government Intervention:** Despite the national security implications (CHIPS Act, semiconductor supply chain), no US government action was taken to secure HPQ supply post-Helene beyond DOE "monitoring."

9. **Alternative Sources:** China produces ~5,000 tons/year (aiming for 20,000). Small production in Norway, Russia, Brazil. Synthetic quartz exists but costs 5-10x more. No viable near-term substitute at scale.

10. **The Carlyle Connection:** Pascal Emsens (Sibelco board, Audit & Sustainability committees) is a former Carlyle Group Managing Director. While he was in renewable energy, not defense, The Carlyle Group's historical intelligence/political connections are notable.
